five recipes making the most of your pantry staples

Hi there! I'm Chef Cat. I love to cook, but as a college student, I am passionate about prioritizing easy, inexpensive, nutritious meals that don't skip on the flavor. Here I've included five of my staple pasta recipes that always have my back. Each recipe comes together in the time it takes to cook up your pasta. Whether you are

red pepper pasta

I have made this recipe multiple times this week. It hits all the marks: minimal ingredients, minimal dishes, maximum flavor and nutrient dense! It comes together in the time it takes to cook the pasta. Here's what you'll need.

  • one roasted red pepper from the jar
  • two big spoonfuls of greek yogurt (I prefer full fat for a more rounded flavor)
  • optional: one spoonful of feta cheese
  • about a tbsp of minced garlic
  • few shakes of onion powder, salt, pepper
  • juice of half a lemon
  • splash of pasta water
  • optional: half tbsp of chili crisp (more or less to your taste)
  • banza protein thin spaghetti noodles: however many your heart desires

Simply cook up your noods and chuck everything else in the blender. Drain noods and throw sauce in pot until everything looks delicious and glossy. I usually have extra sauce which I store in the fridge. Yum!

lemon pesto zucchini pasta

The light and bright pasta that celebrates the flavors of summer. Here's what you will need:

  • about a tbsp of olive oil (graza is my personal favorite)
  • half an onion diced
  • about a tbsp of minced garlic
  • one small zucchini sliced into super thin rounds
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • juice of half a lemon
  • a spoonful of basil pesto (also works great with sundried tomato pesto)
  • splash of pasta water
  • generous amount of freshly grated pecorino romano cheese
  • banza protein thin spaghetti noodles: however many your heart desires

Begin cooking pasta. In a medium sauce pan, sautee onion, garlic, and zucchini in olive oil until zucchini rounds are soft and browned in spots. Season, add lemon juice, and pesto. Add al dente noods, splash of pasta water, and grate on a small mountain pecorino. Finish everything in the pan until glossy and delicious. Devour!

beans and rice

I cannot say enough good things about beans. They are a pantry powerhouse packed with protein and fiber. They are also extremely affordable! To make my life even easier, I love using HeyDay beans because they are packed in a flavorful sauce meaning I can chuck them in a pot and be done. Go check them out! HeyDay beans

  • one can of HeyDay beans (I like the Tomato Alla Vodka Cannellini Beans, Coconut Curry Chickpeas, and Enchilada Black Beans
  • rice or grain of choice (I also like using quinoa or some crusty bread to mop up all those yummy sauces
  • optional: feel free to add some flavor enhancers while these are in the pot (some garlic, squeeze of lemon, chili crisp) get creative and channel your inner chef!

Start cooking your rice in a rice cooker (you can also use the microwave kind). Put your beans in a pot until heated through. Serve over rice and dig in!

miso peanut noodles

Simple ingredients that pack a flavorful punch. These Asian noodles are a little salty, a little sweet, and full of umami. Here's what you will need:

  • one tbsp of white miso paste
  • one tbsp runny peanut butter
  • about a tbsp of minced garlic
  • half tbsp of chili crisp
  • half tbsp of maple syrup or honey
  • half tbsp of rice vinegar
  • two scallions sliced (can omit if you don't have them)
  • about a tbsp of sesame seeds
  • splash of pasta water
  • wheat noodles for a more traditional take or banza protein thin spaghetti for those gains

Begin cooking pasta. In a bowl, combine all other ingredients. Add a splash of pasta water and stir to combine. Add sauce to cooked noodles and stir until combined. Slurp away!

tuna tomato pasta

One of my all time go-to's right here. I almost always have the ingredients to throw this pasta together and they can almost all be found in your pantry. Tuna is where it's at to get a protein + flavor packed meal!

  • one small can of tuna packed in extra virgin olive oil
  • half an onion diced
  • one tbsp of minced garlic
  • a few canned whole tomatoes (save the rest in the fridge)
  • few shakes of chili flakes, salt, pepper
  • juice of half a lemon (maybe more!)
  • splash of pasta water
  • lots of freshly grated pecorino romano (parm works here too)
  • banza protein thin spaghetti noodles: however many your heart desires (can you tell this is my favorite noodle?
    • optional: add castelvetrano olives and capers to give it a puttanesca vibe!

Start cooking the noodles. In a medium sauce pan, sautee diced onion and garlic in olive oil (use the oil from the tin for less waste!) Add whole tomatoes, chili flakes, and seasonings. Add drained tuna. Break everything down with your spatula but leave some chunks! Add lemon juice. Add al dente noods, splash of pasta water, and grate on a small mountain of cheese. Optionally add your olives and capers. Stir until glossy and beautiful! Munch on!
